Hanói Tower

Game About

Legend

There are several legends about the Hanói tower, the most known tell us about a Hindu temple in the center of the universe, where Brahma built a tower with 64 golden discs and two balanced sticks by it's side.

The legend says that Brahma ordered that all the discs had to be moved to the other stick, but only onde disc could be moved at time and a greater disc should never be on top of a smaller one. When it this challenge were complete the temple whould collapse and it would be the end of the world.

Creator

The game was invented in 1883 by the french mathmatician Édouard Lucas, which was inspired by the legends, it's name comes from the tower in Hanói city located in Vietnam

Also known for the Lucas's numbers, sequencie and theorem.

Math

In order to solve the game, there are a way to calculate the minimum movements need to successfull move the tower to the other stick.

The game works in a movement of pieces, but this follow a patter, ir order to move the first, one movement is needed, but for ther second 2 movements are needed since the first disc already is in one stick, so you have to move it in order to put the second in the right place, the same thought is used to the third disc, which has to be moved four times.

Following the patter, you have tom move 2^n-1 times to complete shift the tower.

As example, take the Brahma's problem, that consists of a tower with 64 discs, it would take 18446744073709552000 movements to complete the task.
